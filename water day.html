About World Water Day

World Water Day is an international observance day that is celebrated on March 22nd every year. It was first formally proposed in Agenda 21 of the 1992 United Nations Conference on Environment and Development (UNCED) in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. The United Nations General Assembly designated 22 March as World Water Day in 1993, and it has been held annually since then.

The day focuses attention on the importance of freshwater and advocates for the sustainable management of freshwater resources.

Water Facts

  • Less than 3% of the world's water is fresh water, and only 0.3% is available for human use.
  • More than 2 billion people lack access to safe drinking water.
  • Water scarcity affects more than 40% of the global population and is projected to increase.
  • Water use has been growing at more than twice the rate of population increase in the last century.
  • By 2025, half of the world's population will be living in water-stressed areas.
  • Women and girls are primarily responsible for collecting water in many parts of the world, often walking long distances to do so.
  • Water-related diseases cause 3.4 million deaths each year.
  • Water pollution affects the health of millions of people worldwide.

Water Conservation Tips

There are many simple things we can all do to conserve water:

  1. Fix leaks in faucets and pipes.
  2. Take shorter showers.
  3. Install low-flow showerheads and faucets.
  4. Use a broom instead of a hose to clean driveways and sidewalks.
  5. Water your lawn and garden in the early morning or late evening to reduce evaporation.
  6. Only run full loads in the washing machine and dishwasher.
  7. Collect rainwater for use in your garden.
